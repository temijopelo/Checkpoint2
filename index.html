  • Crispy Coconut Shrimp

    Crispy coconut shrimp is a popular appetizer that combines the sweetness of coconut with the succulent taste of shrimp. Large shrimp are coated in a mixture of seasoned breadcrumbs and shredded coconut, then fried until golden brown and crispy. The result is a crunchy exterior that gives way to tender, juicy shrimp with a hint of tropical flavor. Serve with a sweet and tangy dipping sauce, such as a mango salsa or a chili-lime aioli, for a perfect balance of flavors. This appetizer is sure to transport your taste buds to a sunny beach destination

  • Stuffed Mushrooms

    Stuffed mushrooms are a savory and indulgent appetizer that never fails to impress. Large mushroom caps are filled with a savory mixture typically consisting of breadcrumbs, garlic, herbs, and cheese. They are then baked until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden brown and bubbling. The earthy flavor of the mushrooms pairs perfectly with the rich, savory filling, creating a mouthwatering appetizer that is sure to disappear quickly

Main Courses

  • Grilled Salmon

    Salmon fillet seasoned and grilled to perfection, served with lemon butter sauce.

  • Jollof Rice with Grilled Fish

    Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish made with long-grain rice cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce. The sauce is seasoned with a blend of spices such as onion, garlic, ginger, and chili peppers, giving the rice its distinctive red color and bold flavor. Jollof rice is often served with grilled fish, which is marinated in a mixture of herbs, spices, and citrus juice before being grilled to perfection. The combination of the smoky grilled fish and the spicy, fragrant rice creates a delicious and satisfying meal that is enjoyed across West Africa and beyond.

  • Tagine with Couscous

    Tagine is a traditional Moroccan dish named after the clay pot in which it is cooked. It typically consists of slow-cooked meat, such as lamb or chicken, combined with a variety of vegetables and aromatic spices, including cumin, cinnamon, ginger, and saffron. The dish is cooked slowly over low heat, allowing the flavors to meld together and the meat to become tender and succulent. Tagine is often served with fluffy couscous, which acts as the perfect base for soaking up the flavorful sauce. This dish is a staple of Moroccan cuisine and reflects the rich culinary traditions of the region.

Desserts

  • Chocolate Fondue

    Assorted fruits and marshmallows served with rich chocolate fondue.

  • Mango Sticky Rice

    Mango sticky rice is a popular Thai dessert that features ripe mango slices served with sweet sticky rice, topped with a drizzle of coconut cream. The sticky rice is cooked with coconut milk and sugar, giving it a creamy and sweet flavor. The ripe mangoes are sliced and arranged alongside the sticky rice, providing a refreshing contrast to the rich and creamy rice. The dessert is often garnished with toasted sesame seeds or mung beans for added texture. Mango sticky rice is a delightful combination of flavors and textures—creamy, sweet, and slightly tangy—and is especially popular during the summer months when mangoes are in season.

  • Baklava

    Baklava is a sweet and flaky pastry that is popular in many Middle Eastern and Mediterranean countries. It is made with layers of thin phyllo dough, butter or oil, and a sweet filling typically consisting of chopped nuts (such as walnuts, pistachios, or almonds) and sugar, flavored with spices like cinnamon or cardamom. After layering the ingredients, the pastry is baked until golden brown and crispy, then soaked in a sugar syrup infused with lemon juice or rose water. The result is a deliciously sweet and nutty dessert with layers of delicate pastry that shatter with each bite. Baklava is often served as a dessert or with coffee or tea
